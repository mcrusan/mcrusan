**Marc F. Crusan — Bio**

Marc Crusan is a systems architect, developer, and former U.S. Army Staff Sergeant whose career spans enterprise networking, embedded systems, software engineering, and large‑scale operational leadership. With more than three decades of hands‑on experience across military, government, and private‑sector environments, he has built, secured, and modernized some of the most geographically dispersed and mission‑critical networks in the world.

Marc’s technical foundation is unusually broad: C/C++, Java, Visual Basic, PHP, PERL, SQL, DNS, SMTP, COM++, Linux, UNIX, and Windows internals — paired with deep experience in routers, switches, firewalls, diagnostic tooling, and microcomputer repair. This breadth allowed him to move fluidly between roles as a programmer, systems engineer, network supervisor, consultant, and operations manager.

During his tenure with the Legislative Affairs Agency in Alaska, Marc led network and programming operations across three data centers, supervising both technicians and developers while maintaining uptime, security, and code quality for a massive, distributed infrastructure. His teams delivered production systems in Visual Basic, Java, C++, PHP, and PERL, and he served as the technical backbone for one of the largest dispersed networks in government.

Before that, Marc served as a Senior Programmer/Analyst at Macro*World Research (later Wachovia), where he architected and deployed wireless and push‑alert systems that dramatically improved scalability and reduced server footprint from four machines to one — a rare achievement in that era.

His earlier work includes supporting Red Hat Software as a Linux technician, developing Trilogy‑based systems for IBM/CTG, and running his own computer services company in Alaska, where he built custom hardware and operated a seven‑city ISP.

Marc’s career began in the United States Army, where he served from 1986 to 1998, rising to Staff Sergeant (11B3G). He contributed to the automation of the United Nations Command Security Force, supported the War Fighter project, secured classified systems, trained soldiers, and maintained mission‑critical hardware. His military experience shaped his disciplined approach to systems design, operational reliability, and security.

Marc holds multiple certifications, including A+, Red Hat Linux Administration, Visual Basic Programmer, and advanced programming diplomas in C++ and Java. His work continues to blend engineering rigor with creative problem‑solving, whether he’s building distributed embedded ecosystems, designing secure network architectures, or developing the next generation of AxisOS tooling.
